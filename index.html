THE STUDENT CRISIS IN INDIA
56%
of Students have Learning Gaps
53%
of Students will not be ready for 21st Century Jobs

These challenges exist because students' learning is entirely dependent on teachers, and students have always been a passive participants in the learning process. This is unsustainable because the world is changing and we don't have the teaching infrastructure to support the personalized attention needed.

33:1
Student:Teacher Ratio
HOW DO WE SOLVE THIS CHALLENGE?

We believe that developing agency in students and making them more self-reliant, will reduce the dependence on the adults in the learning process. In order for this to happen, students will need to take ownership of their learning and develop the necessary skill sets for them to thrive. Towards this vision, Involve takes a two pronged appraoch. On one side, through our peer-based programs we equip students with academic and future-ready skills. While on the other side, we work with the larger education landscape to build an ecosystem of trust for our student, empowering them to take stronger ownership of their lives.

AN EMPOWERED STUDENT HAS...
VOICE
Students have spaces for meaningful inclusion in classrooms, schools, communities and governments.
CHOICE
Students have the ability to make choices about their learning, role in society, beliefs and value systems.
ACADEMIC SKILLS
Students have strong foundational academic skills that enable them to make informed choices.
FUTURE READY SKILLS
Students have skills that will help them exercise their voice and contribute meaningfully to the world.
OUR 5 YEAR GOAL

In the next 5 years, we want to develop agency in 1 million students via various programs of peer-based learning with presence in min. 3 states. We also will work towards policy recommendation to have time allocation for Peer-based learning (via curriculum design - Strategies for P2P, Bridge program through PT)
