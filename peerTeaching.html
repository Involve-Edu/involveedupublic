Overview

Our Peer-Teaching program is a 3 year academic and leadership building program. Student Leaders act as tutors for learners from lower grades and aim to bridge their academic gaps. In the process SLs develop future ready skills

OUTCOMES
Academic Outcomes

Students will show academic improvement in both Peer-Teaching classes as well as their own classrooms.

Collaboration

Students will become collaborative learners and be able to work effectively in groups.

Communication

Students will be able to articulate their ideas, thoughts and beliefs orally and in writing.

Ownership

Students will be able to articulate their ideas, thoughts and beliefs orally and in writing.

Problem Solving

Student Leaders will be able to identify and solve problems in various contexts.

Our Model

The first two years of our program require strong operations support. For this purpose we initiated the Changemaker fellowship. Fellows under this program join us part-time to manage Peer teaching operations in schools. These fellows gain exposure into the education sector and get a chance to work in a professional environment while still in college.

Structures
YEAR 1
  • Student Leaders (>Grade 7) and Learners (>Grade 4) are selcted for the program
  • Reflections for SLs, Chapter end tests for Learners
YEAR 2
  • teachers engagement by classroom visits, student conversations
  • Upskilling of SCM to enable them to take ownership of the PTprogram in their schools
YEAR 3
  • Student council members (SCM) work with teachers to run the Peer Teaching program in the school
  • Baseline and Endline tests are conducted to evaluate the program
